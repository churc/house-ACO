This project looks at the locations of Medicare Accountable Care Organizations (ACOs) in the U.S. The ACO model, which started in 2011, is a relatively new health care model. There is a need for more information on the national picture, including demographic details on the ACOs, where they are, who they are serving and who they are not serving. These maps start to lay out an overview of Medicare ACO locations at the national level and start to show these locations in relation to selected health care vulnerability indicators, mapped at a county level.

Background and maps:

In February 2016, CMS listed 433 Medicare ACOs, and at the end of January 2016, Leavitt Partners estimated 838 active ACOs of all kinds (David Muhlestein and Mark McClellan, Accountable Care Organizations In 2016: Private And Public-Sector Growth And Dispersion, April 21, 2016).

Each ACO brings provider participants together to work with each other as a care team. There is a wide variation in the size of the Medicare ACOs, each Medicare ACO is comprised of one or more providers, ranging in size upto 328 providers. The total number of Medicare providers in February 2016 was 14,817 with an average Medicare ACO size coordinating care across 34 providers. Due to data limitations, I used national Medicare data only, the locations do not include state Medicaid, private insurers, Accelerated Development Learning Sessions (ADLS), or other ACO location data.

I did several maps to assess: the differences in locations across years 2014, 2015 and 2016 (map 5), the relation of locations to urban areas (maps 3), and to hospital referral regions (map 2). I looked at the rate of Medicare ACOs by county land area (map 4) by dividing the number of ACOs in each county into the county land area (square meters). Map 6 shows Medicare ACOs by number of providers by color intensity.

The health care indicator maps use the U.S. Census 2014 ACS 5 year data and map rates per county for poverty, high school graduation, disability, 65 years and older, Black or African American alone, and Hispanic or Latino of any race (maps 7 – 11), all of which affect health care outcomes. These investigations led me to focus on poverty and the intersection of mid and high rates of poverty per county on the mainland U.S.A. and Medicare ACOs. Poverty is a known indicator of worse health outcomes and I wanted to explore a national overview of Medicare ACO locations and their relation to financial need. Map 1 analyses poverty rates by county, using spatial analysis in Cartodb and QGIS.

Results:

The 433 Medicare ACOs are spread across 234 counties. Therefore, 2,890 counties have no Medicare ACO (see explanation for 3,124 county total below). This equates as:

  • 7.49% of counties have a Medicare ACO
  • 92.51% of counties do not have a Medicare ACO

The urban area / urban cluster maps spatially joined the Tiger Urban Area dataset and Medicare ACO locations using QGIS: 87% of Medicare ACOs are within urban areas or urban clusters (map 3).

The 2014 ACS 5 year community survey, 'HC03_EST_VC01, percent below poverty level; estimate; population for whom poverty status is determined' has 3,124 entries for counties (it omits State FIPS 60: American Samoa, 66: Guam, 69: Northern Mariana Islands, 72: Puerto Rico, and 78: Virgin Islands of the U.S.). No Medicare ACOs operate in these 5 states, therefore I omitted these states from the maps and worked from the 3,124 county dataset. Dividing county poverty rates (percent below poverty level) by equal intervals gave three groupings:

  • 1% - 18.2% low
  • 18.2% - 35.4% mid
  • 35.4% - 52.6% high

  • 2.2% of high poverty counties (poverty 35.4% - 52.6%) have a Medicare ACO
  • 5.31% of mid poverty counties (poverty 18.2% - 35.4%) have a Medicare ACO
  • 8.73% of low poverty counties (poverty 1% - 18.2%) have a Medicare ACO.

By creating a ten-mile buffer on Medicare ACO locations, and selecting mid-and high-poverty rates (county rates of over 18.2%), map 1 details counties with higher poverty rates which are not within 10 miles of one of the 433 Medicare ACO locations.
